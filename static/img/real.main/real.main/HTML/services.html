Key Communication Skills

Verbal Communication

Clear expression through words, focusing on tone, clarity, and confidence.

  • Use clear, simple language.
  • Avoid filler words like “um” or “you know.”
  • Speak at a moderate pace—neither too fast nor too slow.
  • Match your tone to the message you’re delivering.

Example: Instead of saying, “We might want to consider this,” say, “I recommend we do this because…”

Non-Verbal Communication

Gestures, posture, and expressions that reinforce spoken words.

  • Maintain eye contact to show attentiveness.
  • Smile when appropriate to create warmth.
  • Mirror body language for better connection.

Pro Tip: Be aware of your resting expression—it sends signals even when you’re not speaking.

Body Language

Posture, eye contact, and movements that reflect openness and respect.

  • Stand or sit up straight to show confidence.
  • Avoid closed-off positions like crossed arms.
  • Use open gestures to invite dialogue.

Reminder: People often trust body language more than words when they conflict.

Listening Skills

Active listening to understand and respond thoughtfully.

  • Don’t interrupt the speaker.
  • Show engagement with nods or affirmations.
  • Ask follow-up questions for clarity.
  • Avoid distractions—especially your phone.

Key: People feel valued when they’re heard.

Empathic Reasoning

Understanding others’ emotions and perspectives to build trust.

  • Put yourself in the other person’s shoes.
  • Acknowledge feelings without judgment.
  • Use phrases like “I understand how that could be frustrating.”

Pro Tip: Empathy doesn’t mean agreement—you can understand without necessarily agreeing.
