üìò POSH Act & POSH Policy Explained: A Complete Guide for Indian Workplaces

What is the POSH Act?

Officially the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013, it aims to prevent sexual harassment at the workplace and protect the rights of women employees. Introduced after the Supreme Court‚Äôs 1997 Vishaka Guidelines following Vishaka vs. State of Rajasthan.

Key Objectives of the POSH Act

  • Prevent sexual harassment at work.
  • Protect women in the workplace.
  • Provide a formal redressal mechanism.

What is Sexual Harassment at the Workplace?

  • Unwelcome physical contact or advances.
  • Sexually colored remarks.
  • Showing pornography.
  • Demands for sexual favors.
  • Verbal, written, physical, or non-verbal acts of a sexual nature.

This applies whether the harassment is from colleagues, clients, vendors, or superiors.

Who is Covered Under the POSH Act?

  • All women employees: full-time, part-time, interns, consultants, temporary staff.
  • All workplaces: corporate offices, factories, schools, colleges, hospitals, startups, unorganized sector.
  • All sectors: public and private organizations.

Note: Even work-from-home or remote work scenarios are covered under POSH compliance.

What is a POSH Policy?

A formal internal document that outlines the company‚Äôs stance, procedures, and practices related to preventing and responding to sexual harassment at the workplace.

Elements of a Good POSH Policy

  1. Definition and examples of sexual harassment.
  2. Scope of application (employees, interns, clients, etc.).
  3. Step-by-step complaint process.
  4. Time-bound redressal procedure.
  5. Confidentiality assurance.
  6. Penalties for misconduct or false complaints.
  7. Regular POSH awareness and training programs.

Internal Committee (IC): The Backbone of Compliance

Every organization with 10 or more employees must form an Internal Committee (IC) to handle POSH-related complaints.

Composition of the IC

  • Presiding Officer (senior female employee).
  • Two internal members familiar with legal/social issues.
  • One external member from an NGO/legal background.
  • Minimum 50% of the IC must be women.

Responsibilities of the IC

  • Receive and investigate complaints.
  • Complete the inquiry within 90 days.
  • Submit findings and recommend action.
